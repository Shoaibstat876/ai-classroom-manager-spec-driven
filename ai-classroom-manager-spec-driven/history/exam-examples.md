## Example 1 — Grade 8 Science Test (Rest & Motion)

# Class Test — Science (Kinematics)

**Class:** Grade 8  
**Total Marks:** 25  
**Time Allowed:** 40 minutes  

---

## Instructions
- Attempt all questions.  
- Read each question carefully before answering.  
- Do not use any unfair means during the test.  

---

## Section A — MCQs (5 × 1 = 5 marks)

1. A body is said to be in rest when it:  
   - a) Changes its position quickly  
   - b) Does not change its position with respect to surroundings  
   - c) Moves in a circular path  
   - d) Vibrates about a point  

2. Which of the following is an example of linear motion?  
   - a) Blades of a fan  
   - b) Stone tied to a string and rotated  
   - c) Car moving on a straight road  
   - d) Motion of a clock pendulum  

3. The motion of the hands of a clock is:  
   - a) Linear  
   - b) Random  
   - c) Rotatory  
   - d) Vibratory  

4. A child on a swing shows:  
   - a) Linear motion  
   - b) Vibratory motion  
   - c) Random motion  
   - d) Circular motion  

5. Rest and motion are:  
   - a) Always absolute  
   - b) Relative to the observer  
   - c) Never related  
   - d) Only for moving bodies  

---

## Section B — Short Questions (5 × 2 = 10 marks)

Attempt **all** questions.

1. Define *rest* with one example.  
2. Define *motion* with one example.  
3. What is meant by *rotatory motion*? Give one example.  
4. What is *vibratory motion*? Give one example.  
5. Explain in one or two sentences why rest and motion are called **relative**.  

---

## Section C — Long Questions (2 × 5 = 10 marks)

Attempt **any two** questions.

1. Explain different types of motion (linear, rotatory, vibratory) with suitable examples.  
2. Describe with examples how the same object can be at rest for one observer and in motion for another observer.  
3. Write a short note on the importance of understanding rest and motion in daily life (vehicles, sports, etc.).

## Example 2 — Grade 5 Science Quiz (Water Cycle)

# Quiz — Science (Water Cycle)

**Class:** Grade 5  
**Total Marks:** 20  
**Time Allowed:** 30 minutes  

---

## Instructions
- Attempt all questions.  
- Use simple sentences.  
- Write answers neatly.  

---

## Section A — MCQs (4 × 1 = 4 marks)

1. The process in which water changes into vapour is called:  
   - a) Condensation  
   - b) Evaporation  
   - c) Precipitation  
   - d) Freezing  

2. Clouds are formed due to:  
   - a) Condensation of water vapour  
   - b) Melting of ice  
   - c) Boiling of water  
   - d) Freezing of water  

3. When water falls back to the earth as rain, we call it:  
   - a) Evaporation  
   - b) Condensation  
   - c) Precipitation  
   - d) Filtration  

4. The Sun helps the water cycle mainly by:  
   - a) Cooling water  
   - b) Freezing water  
   - c) Heating water and causing evaporation  
   - d) Stopping evaporation  

---

## Section B — Short Questions (4 × 2 = 8 marks)

Attempt **all** questions.

1. Name the three main stages of the water cycle.  
2. What is *evaporation*?  
3. What is *condensation*?  
4. Why is the water cycle important for life on Earth?  

---

## Section C — Long Questions (2 × 4 = 8 marks)

Attempt **any two** questions.

1. Explain the water cycle in your own words using 4–5 sentences.  
2. Describe what happens to water in each stage: evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.  
3. How does the Sun help the water cycle? Explain with an example from daily life.
